# Encryption and Public Keys

*Placeholder prose. Final prose is written separately after the figure is verified.*

Symmetric encryption uses one shared secret to lock and unlock a message, which
leaves a bootstrapping problem: the secret itself cannot be shared safely over
an open wire. Public-key cryptography solves this with a matched pair of keys,
a public key anyone may have and a private key that is never shared.

Lock a message with someone's public key and only their private key opens it.
Flip the roles and you get signing: lock with your private key, and anyone
holding your public key can open it, which proves the message came from you.
